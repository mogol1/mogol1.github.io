My Work Experience:

I did my Senior Project for high school at Jemicy School. It's a school for kids with dyslexia and other language-based learning differences. Here's what I did while I was there:

  • I taught fifth grade kids how to use Google Drive, Google Docs, and Google Slides so they could make written and visual reports.
  • I reviewed the kids' written work and gave them feedback. I also made suggestions to them on how they can improve their writing.
  • I also taught them how to use iMovie for the iPad to record videos as part of their projects.
  • I tracked where the kids were in their projects, so I could provide suggestions to instructors.
  • Some of the kids wanted to learn how to make websites. So I taught them how to make websites during recess. I think they just didn't want to go outside.

Now I work part-time at the Technology Support Center (TSC) at UMBC. It's a pretty nice job. Here's some of the things I do there:

  • I provide in person and online tech support to members of the UMBC community.
  • I answer the phone if it rings. Usually it's someone who needs help with a computer. So I help them.
  • I record all my interactions with customers, in detail, using our ticketing system.
  • I figure out what kinds of computer problems people are actually having. Sometimes that can be harder than actually fixing the problem.
  • For the tougher problems, my coworkers and I collaborate and figure out what's causing the problem and how we can fix it. Sometimes my supervisors join in on the fun too.
